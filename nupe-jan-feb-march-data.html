3. Insights & Opportunities for Improvement

What Worked Well:

Resend Strategies: Sending follow-ups to engaged users (e.g., "NIKE Thermal RESEND") improved open rates significantly.

Event-Based Marketing: Founders’ Day, Valentine’s Day, and product drops led to spikes in revenue.

Urgency & FOMO Tactics: Emails emphasizing limited availability and restocks performed well.

Personalized Segmentation: Targeting past purchasers of specific items boosted conversions.

Areas for Improvement:

Reduce Overuse of Emojis: Some subject lines relied too much on emojis, which could impact deliverability.

Enhance Product Launches: Some new arrivals had low conversions – consider adding social proof, user testimonials, or stronger CTAs.

Optimize Frequency: While multiple sends per week worked, some niche campaigns (e.g., Atlanta Alumni) may need frequency adjustments.

Refine Abandoned Cart Strategies: Adding more abandoned cart flows could help improve conversion rates.

A/B Test Subject Lines: Testing variations with and without emojis, more personalization, or clearer value propositions may improve open rates further.
